Ancient Art - Art History

The Ancient Art Period

Ancient art refers to the artistic expressions of ancient civilizations, dating back thousands of years. It includes a vast array of styles, techniques, and media from different regions of the world. Some of the most well-known ancient art comes from civilizations like Egypt, Greece, and Rome.

Featured Ancient Civilizations

Ancient Egypt

Ancient Egyptian art dates back to around 3000 BCE and includes a wide range of artistic forms, such as sculptures, paintings, and architecture. Much of their art was created for religious purposes, including decorating tombs, temples, and monuments. Egyptian artists often used a combination of natural pigments and materials, including gold, limestone, and granite.

Ancient Greece

Ancient Greek art, spanning from around 900 BCE to 30 BCE, is renowned for its sculptures, pottery, and architecture. Greek artists developed new techniques and styles, such as the use of perspective and naturalistic depictions of the human body. Greek art often represented religious or mythological themes, as well as scenes from daily life.

Ancient Rome

Ancient Roman art, which began around 500 BCE and lasted until the fall of the Western Roman Empire in 476 CE, was heavily influenced by Greek art but also developed its own unique characteristics. Roman artists created sculptures, frescoes, mosaics, and architecture, often depicting scenes of daily life, historical events, and mythological subjects. Roman art often served as a means of propaganda, promoting the power and authority of the Roman Empire.
