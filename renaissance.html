Renaissance - Art History

The Renaissance Period

The Renaissance was a period of great artistic and intellectual development in Europe, taking place from the 14th to the 17th century. It marked the transition from the medieval period to the Early Modern Age, and it brought about significant advancements in art, architecture, literature, and science. Some of the most famous artists from the Renaissance period include Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael.

Featured Renaissance Artists

Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci was an Italian polymath and a key figure in the Renaissance period. He was a painter, sculptor, architect, scientist, and inventor, and his work has had a lasting impact on various fields. Some of his most famous works include the Mona Lisa, The Last Supper, and the Vitruvian Man.

Michelangelo

Michelangelo was an Italian sculptor, painter, and architect who is considered one of the greatest artists of all time. His most famous works include the statue of David, the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel, and the Last Judgment. Michelangelo's innovative techniques and his mastery of human anatomy have made him an iconic figure in art history.

Raphael

Raphael was an Italian painter and architect who was known for his remarkable clarity of form and ease of composition. His work is admired for its visual achievement of the ideal of human grandeur. Some of his most notable works include The School of Athens, The Transfiguration, and the Madonna series.
